Personal Safety

Tips for staying safe in your daily life

📱 Always Keep Your Phone Charged Basic

Your phone is your lifeline in an emergency. Always ensure it has battery.

  • Keep a portable charger (power bank) in your bag.
  • Save emergency contacts on speed dial.
  • Enable location sharing with trusted family members.
  • Install a safety app like bSafe or Shake2Safety on your phone.

🚶 Walk Confidently and Stay Aware Public

Body language matters. Walk with purpose and keep your surroundings in check.

  • Avoid using headphones at full volume in isolated areas.
  • Walk in well-lit, busy streets whenever possible.
  • Make eye contact briefly — it shows confidence.
  • Trust your instincts. If something feels wrong, move away.

🔑 Self-Defense Basics Safety

Learn basic self-defense techniques and carry legal safety tools.

  • Consider enrolling in a basic self-defense class.
  • Carry a whistle or safety alarm in your bag.
  • Know where to strike in case of attack: eyes, nose, throat, groin, knees.
  • Practice awareness — most attacks can be avoided by spotting danger early.

Travel Safety

Stay safe while commuting and traveling

🚕 Cab & Auto Rickshaw Safety Travel

Follow safety practices every time you book or board a cab.

  • Always verify the cab number and driver photo before getting in.
  • Share your live location with a trusted contact during the ride.
  • Prefer sitting behind the driver, not next to them.
  • Trust app-based cabs (Ola/Uber) over random autos at night.
  • Keep emergency number on speed dial during rides.

🚌 Bus & Public Transport Safety Travel

Buses can be crowded. Know how to stay safe in public transport.

  • Choose seats near the driver or conductor.
  • Stay in well-lit areas of stations and bus stops.
  • If harassed, speak up loudly — public attention deters attackers.
  • Use women-only compartments wherever available.

Online Safety

Protect yourself in the digital world

💻 Protect Your Digital Privacy Online

Cyberstalking and online harassment are real threats. Stay protected.

  • Keep your social media accounts private.
  • Do not share your location, home address, or daily routine publicly.
  • Block and report any user who harasses you online.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for all your accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all platforms.
